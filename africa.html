Africa

Part of

The rifting that took place between North America and Africa produced multiple failed rifts. One of these rifts resulted in the formation of the North Atlantic Ocean. When the minor supercontinent of Gondwana separated into multiple continents, one of them was Africa. The subduction at Tethyan Trench probably caused Africa, India and Australia to move northward, causing the opening of a "South Indian Ocean".

Right now, Australia is inching toward Asia, and the eastern portion of Africa is slowly peeling off from the rest of the continent. Africa is considered by most paleoanthropologists to be the oldest inhabited territory on Earth, with the human species originating from the continent.
